## Example 1: Spotify

```markdown
# Spotify Analysis

## Brief History
Founded in 2006 in Stockholm, Sweden, Spotify revolutionized the music industry by introducing a legal streaming service. The company launched its public service in 2008 and has since grown to become the world's largest music streaming platform.

## Target Audience
- Music enthusiasts of all ages
- Young adults (18-35) as primary demographic
- Commuters and office workers
- Students and young professionals
- People seeking affordable music access

## Core Features
1. Music streaming with extensive library
2. Personalized playlists and recommendations
3. Podcast streaming and creation
4. Cross-platform accessibility

## Unique Selling Points
- Vast music library with over 100 million tracks
- Advanced recommendation algorithms
- Social sharing features
- Offline listening capability
- Free tier with ads

## Business Model
- Freemium model with ad-supported free tier
- Premium subscription ($9.99/month)
- Family and student plans
- Revenue sharing with artists and labels
- Podcast advertising

## Tech Stack Insights
- Backend: Python, Java
- Frontend: React, JavaScript
- Cloud infrastructure: Google Cloud Platform
- Machine learning for recommendations
- Big data processing for analytics

## Perceived Strengths
- User-friendly interface
- Excellent music discovery features
- Strong social integration
- Cross-platform compatibility
- Regular updates and new features

## Perceived Weaknesses
- Artist compensation concerns
- Limited high-quality audio options
- Regional content restrictions
- Premium features behind paywall
- Occasional app performance issues
```

## Brief History
Founded in 2013 by Ivan Zhao, Notion started as a simple note-taking app and evolved into a comprehensive workspace platform. The company gained significant traction in 2018 with its all-in-one workspace concept and has since become a popular tool for teams and individuals.

## Target Audience
- Knowledge workers
- Project managers
- Students and educators
- Remote teams
- Content creators
- Personal productivity enthusiasts

## Core Features
1. Flexible document creation and organization
2. Database and spreadsheet functionality
3. Team collaboration tools
4. Customizable templates

## Unique Selling Points
- All-in-one workspace solution
- Highly customizable interface
- Powerful database capabilities
- Clean, minimalist design
- Cross-platform synchronization

## Business Model
- Freemium model
- Personal Pro plan ($4/month)
- Team plan ($8/user/month)
- Enterprise solutions
- Template marketplace

## Tech Stack Insights
- React for frontend
- Node.js backend
- PostgreSQL database
- Electron for desktop apps
- AWS infrastructure

## Perceived Strengths
- Versatile functionality
- Clean, intuitive interface
- Strong community support
- Regular feature updates
- Excellent template system

## Perceived Weaknesses
- Steep learning curve
- Performance issues with large databases
- Limited offline capabilities
- Mobile app limitations
- Export functionality constraints
